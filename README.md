# Macroeconomic Analysis of Canadian CPI

## Project Summary
This project analyzes Canadian Consumer Price Index (CPI) trends from 2020 to 2024. It explores inflation patterns across time periods and sectors to understand how prices changed during and after major economic disruptions.

## Research Questions
- How did Canadian CPI change from 2020 to 2024?
- What monthly and quarterly inflation patterns were observed?
- Which sectors experienced the highest inflation?
- What factors influenced inflation trends during this period?
- What are the projected CPI trends for 2025 and 2026?

## Dataset Overview
The analysis uses CPI data for Canada from Statistics Canada. The dataset includes monthly, quarterly, and yearly inflation values from 2020 to 2024, along with sector-level data for food, shelter, energy, and other consumer categories.

## Tools and Methods
- Statistics Canada data
- Data cleaning and organization
- Descriptive trend analysis
- Time-based comparison of CPI values
- Sector-wise inflation analysis
- Data visualization for monthly, quarterly, and annual trends
- Basic projection analysis for future CPI growth

## Key Findings
- CPI was relatively stable in 2020, with monthly values around 135.7 to 137.4.
- Inflation rose sharply in 2021 and 2022.
- The CPI peaked at 151.2 in Q2 2022.
- Annual average CPI increased to 157.1 in 2023 and 160.8 in 2024.
- The annual average inflation rate fell from 3.9% in 2023 to 2.4% in 2024.
- Food inflation exceeded 10% in 2022 for store-bought groceries.
- Rental inflation surpassed 8% in 2024.
- Energy prices surged in 2022 but started to plateau by 2024.
- Projected CPI growth is 3.1% in 2025 and 3.0% in 2026.

## Insights
- Inflation was driven by supply chain disruptions, energy shocks, and housing pressure.
- Shelter and energy remained major contributors to higher living costs.
- Policy actions helped stabilize inflation, but prices stayed above pre-pandemic levels.
- The economy showed recovery, but inflationary pressure did not fully disappear.
