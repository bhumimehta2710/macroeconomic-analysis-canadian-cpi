## 🧭 Executive Summary

This report analyzes **Canada's Consumer Price Index (CPI)** from 2020 to 2024 across monthly, quarterly, and annual timeframes — covering the full inflation arc from pandemic suppression to peak crisis and gradual stabilization. Sectoral deep-dives into food, shelter, and energy reveal where Canadians felt the squeeze most, and CPI projections for 2025–2026 provide a forward-looking outlook for policymakers and households.

**Key finding:** CPI peaked at **151.2 in Q2 2022**, driven by Russia-Ukraine conflict and supply chain shocks. By 2024, inflation stabilized at ~3% — but at a permanently higher cost-of-living baseline than pre-pandemic.

## 📈 Key Insights

> 💡 **April 2020 was the historic low (135.7 CPI)** — lockdowns froze demand so effectively that inflation dropped to its lowest point in years, before rebounding sharply as economies reopened.

> 💡 **Rental inflation (8%) outpaced owned housing inflation (6%) in 2024** — urban housing shortages are structurally embedded and unlikely to resolve without sustained policy intervention.

> 💡 **December consistently shows inflation dips** — driven by harvest seasonality (lower produce prices), holiday retail promotions, and in 2024, government tax breaks on food and fuel essentials.

> 💡 **Essentials and non-essentials converged at ~3% by 2024** — a strong signal of normalization, though at a cost-of-living level significantly above pre-pandemic baselines.

> 💡 **Store-bought food inflation exceeded 10% in 2022**, far outpacing restaurant meal inflation — supply chain disruption hit grocery retail harder than the food service sector.

## 🔮 Projections (2025–2026)

Inflation is projected to continue growing at a controlled pace — **+3.1% in 2025** and **+3.0% in 2026** — reflecting effective monetary policy. Key risks to watch:

- Geopolitical disruptions affecting energy markets
- Persistent housing supply shortfalls in urban centers
- Global supply chain re-shocks
- Bank of Canada rate adjustment timing

---

## 💼 Policy Implications

- **Housing** — Rental inflation at 8% demands sustained affordable housing investment; short-term fixes will not resolve structural urban shortages
- **Energy** — Clean energy transition investments directly reduced CPI exposure to volatile international fuel markets
- **Food Security** — Supply chain resilience programs are needed to prevent grocery inflation spikes during future global disruptions
- **Monetary Policy** — Rate hikes proved effective in cooling the 2022 peak; forward guidance must balance growth with price stability
